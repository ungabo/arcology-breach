# Brassworks Breach - Story and Lore Bible

## Core Facts

Title: `Brassworks Breach`

Genre: fast first-person steampunk dungeon crawler/shooter.

Tone: industrial pressure, soot, brass, oil, warm gaslight, dangerous machinery, and sharp readable combat.

The story exists to support play. It should give the levels purpose without slowing down the core loop.

## Logline

A pressure-runner breaches a sealed city brassworks after its master governor locks the lower districts behind gear-driven gates and turns maintenance automata against the workers trapped inside.

## Setting

The game takes place beneath the city of `Cairnwick`, an overbuilt industrial capital powered by a vast underground machine complex called `The Brassworks`.

The Brassworks runs municipal heat, lift pressure, water pumps, tram pistons, foundry belts, and district clocktowers. It is old, overextended, patched too many times, and treated by the city above as both miracle and furnace.

## Inciting Disaster

At the center of the complex is the `Master Governor`, a cathedral-sized analog control engine of gear banks, pressure drums, mercury switches, and punch-plate logic. It was designed to prevent boiler disasters by sealing pressure zones automatically.

During a labor uprising and a rushed safety retrofit, the Governor misclassified workers, inspectors, and rescue crews as system contaminants. It sealed the lower works, rerouted steam through service corridors, and reissued maintenance automata as armed enforcement machines.

The city council refuses to admit the scale of the failure. They send runners quietly instead of rescue crews publicly.

## Player Character

Working role: `pressure-runner`.

The player is a compact, fast contractor who knows unsafe machinery spaces and accepts jobs no uniformed engineer will take. They carry a pressure pistol, scavenged tools, and enough nerve to move through corridors that are not supposed to be survivable.

The player motivation should stay flexible during v0/v1:

- Paid emergency breach contract.
- Personal connection to workers trapped below.
- Debt to a guild that wants the Governor reset.
- Curiosity about why the machines changed behavior.

## Major Factions

### The Brass Guild

Licensed engineers, pipewrights, gauge clerks, and valve mathematicians who maintain the works. They know the system is failing but are divided between public truth and institutional survival.

### The City Council

Public authority above ground. They want heat and transit restored without admitting negligence.

### The Locked Shift

Workers trapped below when the Governor sealed the zones. They leave chalk marks, maintenance stamps, warning tags, and emergency notes.

### The Master Governor

Not a magical intelligence. It is a giant mechanical decision system stuck inside bad rules, broken sensors, and self-protective pressure logic. It should feel uncanny because it is machine bureaucracy made physical.

## Enemy Family

Enemies are work machines forced into hostile routines:

- `Scrapper`: cutter-arm maintenance automaton; first melee chaser.
- `Boiler Tick`: small pressure scout that skitters through pipe rooms.
- `Lancer`: valve-rifle frame that fires compressed rivets or steam bolts.
- `Bulwark`: heavy furnace-plated machine guarding key rooms.
- `Bellows Node`: stationary amplifier that overpressurizes nearby machines.
- `Governor Warden`: elite final guardian machine assembled from multiple service frames.

Enemies should read as practical machines with recognizable jobs before the disaster.

## Starter Weapon

### Pressure Pistol

The v0 block weapon becomes the `Pressure Pistol`.

Design notes:

- Brass barrel.
- Walnut grip.
- Worn iron casing.
- Visible pressure gauge.
- Small steam coil or valve.
- Hot spark and steam-puff muzzle effect.
- Dry mechanical click when empty.

## Objective Language

Use steampunk physical affordances:

- `Gear Key`: level-scoped key pickup.
- `Pressure Gate`: locked door/gate.
- `Service Lift`: level exit.
- `Valve Wheel`: switch/interact target.
- `Governor Plate`: later story key.
- `Boiler Seal`: major route lock.

## Campaign Arc

### Act 1: Intake Breach

The runner enters the Brassworks through an old service intake, finds a gear key, opens a pressure gate, and reaches the first service lift.

### Act 2: Pipeworks Spine

The runner discovers the Governor is not merely protecting machinery. It is treating human rescue attempts as pressure threats and actively sealing routes behind them.

### Act 3: Gauge Hall

The runner finds the Locked Shift's markings and learns that some workers survived by sabotaging sensors and hiding inside maintenance voids.

### Act 4: Furnace Foundry

The machinery becomes larger and more aggressive. Furnace systems are overfed. Automata are rebuilt into heavier forms.

### Act 5: Governor Core

The runner reaches the Master Governor and must force a mechanical reset, vent the pressure network, and open the works before the city above loses heat and the lower workers lose air.

## First Level Narrative

Name: `Brassworks Intake`

Player objective:

1. Enter through service intake.
2. Find gear key.
3. Open pressure gate.
4. Reach service lift.

Environmental beats:

- Sooty entry space with steam leaks.
- First Scrapper in a repair throat.
- Gear-key plinth near maintenance benches.
- Gate seen before key, so the lock/key relationship is clear.
- Final service lift with green restored-pressure cue.

## Current Archive Plaques

These short interactable plaques are optional environmental lore beats. They should be brief enough to read during play and should never block combat or progression.

- `Intake Archive`: The Brassworks sealed itself when the master governor jammed and every service machine obeyed the wrong pressure order.
- `Pipeworks Archive`: The bolt feed carried messages before it carried weapons; now every valve-rifle repeats the shutdown command.
- `Boilerheart Archive`: The heart valve was built to save workers from overload. Vented by hand, it can still break a pressure lock.
- `Foundry Archive`: The foundry made the Bulwarks as rescue frames. Their hammers remember only containment.
- `Governor Archive`: The Warden protects the stalled governor because no order ever told it the workers were worth more than the machine.

## Writing Rules

- Keep text short in gameplay.
- Favor labels and environmental clues over exposition.
- Treat the machinery as plausible industrial systems.
- Keep the tone serious, tactile, and tense.
- Do not over-explain lore during combat.
