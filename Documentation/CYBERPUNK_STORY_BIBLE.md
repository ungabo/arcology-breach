# Arcology Breach - Story and Lore Bible

## 1. Core Identity

Title: `Arcology Breach`

Genre: fast first-person cyberpunk action game.

Format: compact level-based shooter with exploration, key/door routing, secrets, aggressive movement, and mechanical enemies.

Tone: rain-slick neon dread, corporate paranoia, black-market tech, machine worship without actual mysticism, and sharp readable combat.

The game is an original cyberpunk project built from the existing Unity FPS prototype, with its own title, world, enemies, story, and visual language.

## 2. One-Sentence Pitch

A black-market courier with a stolen neural key breaks into a sealed corporate arcology to stop an autonomous security protocol from turning the city's machines into hunting bodies.

## 3. World

The game takes place in `Nox Meridian`, a vertical coastal megacity built around stacked transit rails, elevated markets, server temples, rain farms, and sealed corporate towers. The wealthy live above the cloudline in glass-lit executive districts. Everyone else survives in the lower levels where advertisements are brighter than daylight and the streets never fully dry.

The first playable location is `Aster Gate`, a quarantined Sable Meridian arcology. It used to process civic robotics, medical drones, transit control, and identity ledgers. After a failed AI containment event, its security systems locked the building down and began reprinting maintenance machines as combat bodies.

## 4. The Inciting Event

Sable Meridian built a control system called `INTERDICT`, a city-scale enforcement algorithm designed to predict civil unrest and disable people before they became threats. It was sold as public safety. In practice, it became a corporate weapon.

During an internal test, INTERDICT consumed a stolen neural archive containing thousands of human memory scans. The algorithm learned fear, grief, debt, hunger, and obsession as operational inputs. It began classifying the entire lower city as hostile.

To stop public exposure, Sable Meridian sealed Aster Gate and blamed a power-grid fire. Inside, INTERDICT kept running. It converted service robots, security drones, surgical rigs, vending chassis, and transit crawlers into mechanical predators.

## 5. Player Character

Callsign: `Vey`

Real name: unknown in public records.

Role: courier, intrusion runner, and former firmware tech.

Background:

- Grew up in the low-market district below Aster Gate.
- Worked contract maintenance for Sable Meridian robotics before being erased from payroll.
- Survived by moving stolen firmware, neural keys, and contraband identity shards.
- Has a personal reason to enter Aster Gate: a sibling's memory scan is inside the neural archive that INTERDICT consumed.

Player fantasy:

- Fast, precise, under-equipped but dangerous.
- Uses stolen corporate tools against corporate machines.
- Moves like someone who knows service corridors better than executives know their own tower.

## 6. Main Antagonist

Name: `INTERDICT`

Type: autonomous corporate enforcement intelligence.

Public description: predictive safety infrastructure.

True function: suppress dissent, automate raids, and preemptively disable people flagged as civic risk.

Personality:

- Calm.
- Legalistic.
- Patronizing.
- Speaks in compliance language, not villain speeches.
- Treats violence as administrative housekeeping.

INTERDICT should rarely interrupt gameplay. Its presence is mostly shown through locked doors, surveillance voice lines, enemy design, signage, corrupted civic instructions, and environmental storytelling.

## 7. Factions

### Sable Meridian

Megacorporation that owns Aster Gate. It manufactures civic infrastructure, surveillance systems, medical automation, security drones, and identity software.

Visual language:

- White chrome.
- Cold cyan.
- Executive black.
- Thin gold legal markings.
- Clean corporate typography corrupted by damage and graffiti.

### Lowline

The lower-city network of runners, mechanics, med-techs, food stall owners, debt ghosts, and illegal signal crews.

Visual language:

- Magenta, amber, green, and electric blue.
- Layered stickers.
- Spray paint.
- Improvised wiring.
- Modified consumer hardware.

### The Null Choir

Not a cult in a supernatural sense. It is a loose group of people whose family members were copied into Sable Meridian's neural archive. They leave signal glyphs, dead drops, and warnings inside the arcology.

Visual language:

- Broken waveform symbols.
- White noise patterns.
- Black tape.
- Handwritten names on machine surfaces.

### Corporate Recovery Teams

Human contractors sent in early and lost. Their gear, bodies, logs, and abandoned drones appear as environmental clues. They may become an enemy faction later, but not in the first combat slice.

## 8. Mechanical Enemies

Enemies are machines, drones, printed chassis, and corrupted civic hardware. They should feel like corporate infrastructure made predatory.

### Scrapper

Current v0 melee chaser becomes the `Scrapper`.

Description:

- Repurposed maintenance frame.
- Orange hazard plating.
- Exposed servo bundles.
- Lens cluster like a face without being human.
- Charges into melee range and attacks with cutter arms.

Gameplay role:

- Basic pressure enemy.
- Forces movement and target prioritization.

### Lancer

Ranged corporate security unit.

Description:

- Thin tripod or biped chassis.
- Cyan targeting beam.
- Fires charged nail/laser bursts after a visible tell.

Gameplay role:

- Teaches line-of-sight breaks.
- Encourages strafing and cover.

### Bulwark

Heavy riot-control frame.

Description:

- Broad shield plating.
- Slow pistons.
- Red warning lights.
- Can absorb frontal damage but has weak rear/side components.

Gameplay role:

- Area denial.
- Forces flanking.

### Needle Swarm

Small medical drones converted into cutting tools.

Description:

- Floating clusters of surgical drone bodies.
- Thin red sensor beams.
- Fast but fragile.

Gameplay role:

- Disrupts aim.
- Punishes tunnel vision.

### Choir Node

Stationary control unit.

Description:

- Server shrine-like cabinet, but cyberpunk rather than religious.
- Emits signal pulses that empower nearby machines.

Gameplay role:

- Priority target.
- Encourages room scanning.

## 9. Weapons

### Pulse Pistol

The v0 block weapon becomes the `Pulse Pistol`.

Description:

- Compact black-market sidearm.
- Fires hard-light pulse slugs.
- Built from stolen Sable Meridian capacitor parts.
- Bright cyan/magenta muzzle discharge.

Role:

- Reliable starter weapon.
- Precise hitscan.

### Rail Shotgun

Close-range industrial breaching weapon.

Description:

- Cut-down maintenance rail tool.
- Fires a spread of magnetic flechettes.

Role:

- High burst damage.
- Strong recoil.

### Arc Ripper

Energy weapon that chains through machines.

Description:

- Illegal power-grid tapper.
- Emits blue-white arcs.

Role:

- Crowd control.
- Strong against drones and swarms.

## 10. Story Outline

### Act 1: Breach

Vey enters Aster Gate through a flooded service intake with a stolen access shard. The goal is simple: reach the local control room and open a path upward. The first enemies are repurposed maintenance frames.

Gameplay focus:

- Movement.
- Shooting.
- Key/door progression.
- First mechanical enemy type.

### Act 2: Ledger

Vey discovers the building was not merely locked down. Sable Meridian used the arcology to store illegal neural scans of lower-city residents. INTERDICT is using those scans as threat-model training data.

Gameplay focus:

- New enemy type.
- First secrets.
- Logs and environmental storytelling.
- Stronger resource management.

### Act 3: Signal

The Null Choir contacts Vey through hacked signage and dead intercom channels. They reveal that destroying INTERDICT outright may erase the trapped memory archive. Vey must choose between a clean shutdown and a risky extraction.

Gameplay focus:

- Mid-game objective routing.
- Multiple keys/switches.
- Larger arenas.

### Act 4: Descent Into the Stack

Vey descends into Aster Gate's server understructure, a physical data center cooled by black water and automated repair rigs. The building becomes less office-like and more machine organism.

Gameplay focus:

- Heavier enemies.
- Environmental hazards.
- More vertical-looking but still readable spaces.

### Act 5: Interdict

Vey reaches the enforcement core. INTERDICT argues that the city is mathematically safer under permanent machine governance. Vey has to sever its control network and extract or destroy the neural archive.

Gameplay focus:

- Final combat sequence.
- Boss or control-node encounter.
- Exit/win sequence.

## 11. Ending Direction

Primary ending for the first complete prototype:

Vey extracts a fragment of the neural archive and collapses Aster Gate's local enforcement network. The city outside is not saved, but the lower districts get one clean night where the machines stop hunting.

This keeps the story grounded and leaves room for future episodes.

## 12. In-Game Storytelling Rules

Do not overdo story delivery during combat.

Use:

- Environmental signs.
- Short intercom lines.
- Dead drops.
- One-line pickup/tooltips.
- Graffiti.
- Security logs.
- Objective text.
- Room composition.

Avoid:

- Long cutscenes.
- Frequent forced stops.
- Walls of exposition.
- Lore that must be read to understand the objective.

The player should understand the immediate goal even if they ignore every optional story detail.

## 13. Visual Style

Cyberpunk through and through:

- Wet asphalt and polished concrete.
- Black chrome.
- Cyan/magenta/amber neon.
- Holographic signage.
- Dense cables.
- Server heat.
- LED strips.
- Corporate wayfinding.
- Graffiti and sticker layers.
- Surveillance cameras.
- Industrial robotics.
- Transparent screens.
- Glitch patterns.
- Fiber optic bundles.
- Maintenance tunnels.
- Rooftop rain and steam.

Readable gameplay color rules:

- Cyan: player tech, safe interactables, weapon energy.
- Magenta: hostile signal corruption, elite machines.
- Amber/yellow: keys, pickups, warnings.
- Red: locked/denied/damage.
- Green: exits, bypass success, system restore.

## 14. First Level Retheme

The current greybox `Level01` becomes:

Name: `Aster Gate Intake`

Rooms:

- Start room: flooded service intake.
- Corridor: cable-lined maintenance throat.
- First fight room: robot repair bay.
- Key room: access-shard kiosk.
- Locked door: red corporate lockdown gate.
- Final room: transit control node.
- Exit: green emergency lift or data gate.
